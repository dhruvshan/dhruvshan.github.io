The story behind the MechDex Pro was inspired by the by the human ingenuity of turning Macbook Pro's without screens into a full on laptop/desktop conversion, just with the help of a HDMI cable.

However, in an interest to create something different and inspired by Youtube DIY God, DIY Perks, I used the most unique feature available in Samsung's smartphone: Dex. Combining both these concepts was really the genesis of the MechDex Pro. Built using acrylic, a custom 40% mechanical keyboard, a simple USB-C hub and of course a powerbank to power it all.


The beauty behind the project, as because it was a phone, you could simply use your phone as usual and say when you wanted have access to a bigger screen or do actual productive work, you could just plug it in to this accessory and ta da, a light, cheap, portable laptop.
The iPad Mini 6 is a wonderful little iPad that truly showcases what the iPad was meant to be: a small portable device for media consumption, note taking and the occasional gaming

However, iPad's of all sizes have become a lot more powerful and are sometimes used as productivity machines. Sadly Apple's best iPad productivity accessory, the Magic Keyboard, wasn't built for the Mini. So I decided to build one myself. Built using acrylic, a custom 40% mechanical keyboard, a simple USB-C hub, and cheap knockoff iPad Mini 'Smart' case I was able to transform the iPad into perhaps Apple's smallest laptop.


It is admittedly pretty thick, but it probably has one of the best typing experiences out there. And doesnt it just look really cool!
